## Section 2 — What Reachr Is

**The product:** India's version of Whop's Content Rewards. A performance distribution marketplace combining a UGC marketplace + content distribution platform. Brands get content created AND distributed through a network of creators and clippers.

**How it works:**
- Brands set a campaign budget and a reward rate per 1,000 views
- Clippers join campaigns and create brand new accounts with zero prior following
- Clippers use brand-provided assets to produce YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels
- Clippers get paid based on verified views generated
- Views redirect to wherever the brand specifies — social account, website, product page

**The core problem it solves:** D2C brand content doesn't compound. Every campaign feels like starting from zero. Brands pay for UGC AND paid reach separately — paying twice to reach the same audience.

**Stage:** Pre-revenue, building phase (April 2026)

**The tagline / positioning:** "Good content dies every day. Not because it's bad. Because distribution was an afterthought."

**The unified thesis:**
- Half 1 (diagnosis): Content without distribution is noise. Algorithms are landlords — you rent reach, never own it.
- Half 2 (bridge): Owned channels compound, but brands need distribution infrastructure to reach those channels in the first place. Reachr is that infrastructure.

### 5 Root Cause Insights from the Data

Post 13 is the highest-quality post AND the only one that got engagement from strangers. Write 1 post/week about LinkedIn itself. The reader is already on LinkedIn — the topic is immediately relevant to their daily reality.

Every post argues "you're renting reach, build owned distribution." Same argument, 15 variations. New angles needed — not new wrappers for the same point.

**Insight 4 — No personal posts in 15 posts:**
100% authority/educational content. No founder story, no personal observation, no behind-the-scenes. LinkedIn rewards parasocial trust. Without personal posts, it's a newsletter, not a person.

**Insight 5 — Network isn't growing:**
Not sending daily connection requests to ICP. Without targeted network growth (15–20/day to D2C founders + growth operators), content reach ceiling stays at ~200 regardless of quality.

## Section 5 — The Metric Hierarchy (DO NOT CHANGE)

At sub-1K followers, the correct priority order is:

1. **Comments in first 60 mins** — THE only signal that triggers algorithmic amplification
2. **Follows gained from a post** — Evidence the content made someone want more
3. **Profile visits from a post** — Intent signal
4. **Likes/Reactions** — Secondary signal, doesn't drive distribution much
5. **Impressions** — Lagging indicator. Goes up when comments improve. NOT the primary target.

**Do not optimize for impressions. Optimize for comments. Impressions follow.**

### Post Quality Rules (Non-Negotiable)

**Hook rules:**
- Max 120 characters — must fit before "...see more" on mobile
- FACT + TENSION structure (not just a fact — strip the tension and check what's left)
- 1 line only (Jasmin Alić: hooks over 1 line perform 20% worse)
- Never start with: "I", "Unpopular opinion:", "Here's the thing:", proper noun alone
- Write 3 variants, pick the sharpest. Body first, hook mined last.

**The 6 tension types for hooks:**
1. Result + Missing Ingredient — "Snitch crossed ₹900 Cr without a single celebrity deal."
2. Belief + Contradicting Number — "Everyone says organic reach is dead. This brand got 4M impressions free."
3. Action + Hidden Cost — "Every boosted post pays for reach that forgets you in 48 hours."
4. Scale + Counterintuitive Source — "Their best month came from 12 creators with under 10K followers."
5. Investment + What Kills It — "6 months to 50K followers. You own none of them."
6. Standard Advice + Missing Piece — "Agencies tell you to post daily. None tell you who owns the audience."

**Structure rules:**
- Every paragraph = 1–2 sentences max
- Double line break between every paragraph
- No Twitter-poetry (1–3 word lines on their own)
- 800–1,500 characters sweet spot for text posts
- Body first, hook mined last

**CTA rules:**
- Must be publicly answerable in 5–10 words
- Must create mild social pressure or invite comparison/debate
- Never use: "What do you think?", "Share below", abstract philosophical questions
- Good formats: "Drop a ✓ if X", "Has your brand ever Y?", "Which of these is your team doing right now — A or B?"

**Spiky POV requirement:**
Every post must complete: "Most D2C founders believe [X]. But [Y] is actually true."
If nobody would disagree, sharpen it. Polite agreement = no comments = no algorithm distribution.

**AI pattern check (before every post — humanizer catches these):**
- Em dash overuse (max 1 per post)
- "Tapestry", "landscape", "foster", "underscore", "showcase", "pivotal", "delve"
- Overly balanced paragraph structure
- Rule of three overuse
- Negative parallelisms ("It's not just X — it's Y")

### Weekly Posting Rhythm (4x/week, not 7x)

Daily posting with 0 engagement trains the algorithm to ignore the account. 4 high-quality posts beats 7 mediocre ones.

| Day | Bucket | Format | Audience Source |
|-----|--------|--------|----------------|
| Mon | Growth | Brandjack — specific Indian D2C brand event | Brand's existing audience |

*The Mistake Post:* "I spent 3 weeks posting about D2C distribution and got 0 comments. Here's what I was doing wrong." Highest-trust post type at Stage 1. Shows self-awareness. Invites comments. Inherently spiky. Use every 2–3 weeks.

## Section 9 — The GTM Strategy (Full, Two-Track)

## Section 14 — The Most Important Reframe

The old strategy was: LinkedIn → audience → customers. That's a 12–18 month path to revenue.

**The correct sequence:** get customers → use results as content → content amplifies sales.

```
Direct Sales ──── ENGINE  (generates revenue + proof)
     │
     ▼
Content ────────── FUEL    (amplifies sales, builds trust, creates inbound)
     │
     ▼
Dashboard ──────── PANEL   (measures everything, prevents repeats, tracks pipeline)
```

Customers come first. Content supports them. The dashboard measures both.

## Section 16 — What NOT to Do (Mistakes Already Identified)

- Do NOT score posts by impressions alone — engagement rate matters more
- Do NOT agree when Sumanth says a post performed well without checking the data
- Do NOT reuse the same CTA across multiple posts
- Do NOT write Twitter-poetry style (1–3 word lines) — feels like AI on LinkedIn
- Do NOT start hooks with proper nouns alone ("RAJ SHAMANI", "Seeaash")

- Do NOT write posts that only agree with the reader — spiky POV needed to generate comments

- Do NOT start cold outreach before having clippers ready
- Do NOT treat LinkedIn content as the primary revenue driver — it supports sales, it doesn't replace it
